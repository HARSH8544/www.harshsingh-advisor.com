Why Mental Health Matters

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Top 10 Common Mental Health Questions

1. What is mental health?

It refers to cognitive, behavioral, and emotional well-being. It is all about how people think, feel, and behave.

2. How do I know if I'm unwell?

Common signs include prolonged sadness, excessive worry, extreme mood changes, and withdrawal from friends/activities.

3. Can stress make me physically sick?

Yes. Chronic stress can lead to headaches, stomach issues, high blood pressure, and chest pain.

4. How can I improve my mood naturally?

Regular exercise, a balanced diet, sufficient sleep, and sunlight can all naturally boost serotonin levels.

5. What is anxiety?

Anxiety is your body's natural response to stress. It's a feeling of fear or apprehension about what's to come.

6. When should I see a therapist?

If your emotions are overwhelming, interfering with your daily life, or if you are using harmful coping mechanisms.

7. Is depression curable?

Depression is treatable. Most people see significant improvement with therapy, medication, or a combination of both.

8. How does sleep affect mental health?

Poor sleep is linked to depression and anxiety. Sleep allows the brain to process emotions and recharge.

9. What is "Mindfulness"?

Mindfulness is the practice of being fully present and engaged in the moment, without judgment.

10. How can I help a friend?

Listen without judgment, encourage them to seek professional help, and simply be there to support them.
